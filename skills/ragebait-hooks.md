# Ragebait Hooks

## What This Skill Does
Generates scroll-stopping TikTok hooks that trigger emotional responses — jealousy, curiosity, controversy.

## When To Use
- Creating TikTok/Instagram content for education apps
- When standard motivational hooks are underperforming (<300 views)
- When you need comments and shares, not just views

## When NOT To Use
- Brand-sensitive accounts that can't be controversial
- Audiences that respond better to aspirational content
- When the account is brand new (build trust first)

## The Formula

```
Line 1-2: SHOCKING personal claim (triggers scroll-stop)
           Use CAPS on exactly 2 keywords
Line 3-4: Relatable contrast (ChatGPT vs your app)
Line 5-6: Product mention (casual, like telling a friend)
Line 7-8: Ending that triggers comments ("now they think...")
```

## Examples That Worked
- "everyone in my school keeps calling me the TAYLOR SWIFT of MATH. i just stopped using ChatGPT and started using the Professor Curious app." → 450 views
- "i used to be the DUMBEST PERSON in my class. then i found the Professor Curious app. now my teacher thinks i'm cheating." → 380 views

## Examples That Failed
- "she went from failing chemistry to getting an A in 3 weeks. her secret? Professor Curious" → 180 views (felt fake, not personal)
- "STOP copying from ChatGPT. Professor Curious teaches you HOW" → 200 views (too preachy)
